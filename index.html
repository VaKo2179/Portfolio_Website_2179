about me

i am a BugBountyHunter

I won’t bore you with the usual “I went to school” story. Instead, I want to express my deep passion and desire to be good enough in Linux to say, "Hey, I use Arch, by the way." So, this is my current Linux philosophy: to have Arch installed on bare metal as my main operating system, making it crash a thousand times while running virtual machines on it and pulling my hair out. I’ve found this to be the best way to deal with the problems.

Here comes the boring part—feel free to skip to the next tab! My journey began five years ago when I stumbled upon an online bootcamp, where I learned HTML, CSS, JavaScript, Python, and some basics of networking. I briefly attended college to extend my knowledge and earned a degree in backend development, after which I took on some small online projects. However, I soon realized that something was not quite right; I enjoyed working in the terminal but not building websites. My lecturer advised me to try Ubuntu, which sparked my interest in the command prompt. I delved deeper into Linux, exploring some fundamental principles and familiarizing myself with the file system. I also read some books, including "The Linux Command Line" (first and second editions) by William Shotts, which was really inspiring and motivated me to learn about other distributions until I finally settled on Arch. So, for the last few years, I have been traveling with my laptop and playing around with Linux, but I have never worked for a company. Now, I am based in Georgia and ready to work.

I desire to become a bug bounty hunter, but I am open to any Linux-related jobs, such as system administration, DevOps, network administration, cybersecurity, and technical support, to expand my knowledge. Additionally, I am open to any open-source projects or collaborations.
